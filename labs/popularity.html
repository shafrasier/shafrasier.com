Method. Counts come from the Discogs monthly data dump (July 1, 2026). Recordings counts master releases — Discogs collapses every reissue of a work onto its earliest release year, so the curve approximates when music was made. Pressings counts every release at its own year, reissues included — RYM's semantics. "Share of all output" divides each year by the database's total for that year, so a genre's dominance is read against its era rather than against the CD-boom's sheer volume.

Caveats found while building. Masters undercount the pre-LP era: 78s often carry no master, and early catalogs entered Discogs as CD compilations (masters-Delta-blues "peaks" in 1991). The pressings series is the honest one for anything before the fifties. Coverage skews toward electronic and rock; country and gospel run thin. Style names are Discogs vocabulary and map onto the MAP's genres nearly 1:1 ("Bop" for bebop is the odd one out).

If adopted. A script in the GEARS re-derives one JSON from the latest dump (36 seconds for masters, ~15 minutes for pressings, no API keys, no scraping); a transformer renders the row from a style→genre mapping in each note's frontmatter. Refresh quarterly with the deploy.
