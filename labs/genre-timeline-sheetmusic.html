Sheet-music timeline — feature lab

The genre timeline already reads as a staff: spines, note-heads, barlines, slurs. Here are seven ways to lean further into the sheet-music metaphor — toggle each on and off to see what you like. Nothing here is live on the MAP; it's a sandbox.

Sheet-music features

The seven: ① Clef — a treble clef opening each staff. ② Time signature — a stacked meter after the clef (chosen to evoke each genre's feel). ③ Tempo / character marking — an italic Italian direction above each staff (Delta lento, pesante; Piedmont allegretto, dolce; Chicago con fuoco). ④ Note durations — open vs. filled heads with stems/flags, encoding lifespan. ⑤ Repeat barlines — ‖: … :‖ bracketing a genre's active years. ⑥ Brace — a grand-staff { tying an umbrella genre to its children. ⑦ Crescendo / decrescendo hairpins — under genres that swelled or faded.
