Moments

Single days that split the music's history into before and after. Genres get lines — durations, lineages. A moment gets a point: a flashbulb on the wire.

Design rationale

The Genres landing already owns the timeline-as-duration: long bars that overlap, branch, and persist. If Moments borrowed that form, a one-day event would render as a sliver — a degenerate bar — and the category would read as "very short eras," which is exactly wrong. A moment's significance isn't how long it lasted; it's that history is discontinuous at that point. So this page draws points, not spans: one wire across the years, each moment a flashbulb burst on it. Lots of cream, very few bursts — the scarcity is deliberate, because a Moment has to earn its way onto the wire.

The hinge is the category's editorial argument made visual. Every Moments note — Newport is the proof — is structured as before/after: what the world still assumed on the morning of that day, and what stopped being assumable by midnight. Selecting a burst opens the moment as a hinge: "before" set in receding gray and aligned toward the fold, "after" in full ink, the burst at the crease between them. That framing doubles as the admission test for the category: if you can't write the before sentence and the after sentence, the event is trivia, not a Moment.

Scaling lives in the geometry, not the markup. Positions are computed from the date, so adding a moment is one entry in a data array; date flags dodge collisions by alternating sides of the wire and stepping their stem heights — Sgt. Pepper and Monterey, twenty-one days apart, already exercise this. Below 700px the wire rotates vertical and hinges expand in place, so nothing breaks at 380px. The horizontal wire stays legible to roughly thirty bursts; past that, the same date data drives a decade-zoomed wire without touching a single note.
